Throw a CryptoParty

The more crypto the better! Throw your own party!

Hosting a Cryptoparty can be an extremely fun and informative way to engage your community in a conversation about how we can protect ourselves against digital surveillance. Cryptoparties teach valuable skills and raise awareness for electronic privacy issues while fostering an open social atmosphere. It is generally recommended that you begin to plan the party at least one month in advance in order to ensure venue availability and sufficient time for planning and advertising.

Nutshell

  1. Find and reserve a venue that will meet your needs. Capacity, Wi-Fi, and food and drink availability are the most important factors.
  2. Select a topic for the party, and ensure you have someone to present it and others to help your guests with the software.
  3. Get the word out through flyers, social media, newspapers, and event sites.
  4. Set a party schedule and ensure you have all the supplies you need.
  5. Party like it's 1984!

PRE-PARTY PREPARATION CHECKLIST

Venue

 Establish reliable wifi connections at event
 Visit site beforehand to be familiar with seating, outlets, wifi, and A/V setup
 Ensure food and drinks are available
 Reserve the venue as far in advance as possible to show on advertisements

Advertise event

 Social media: Facebook, Twitter, Reddit
 E-mail notice in newsletter to mailing list subscribers
 Contact potentially allied groups with invitations
 List the event in city calendars, local event sites, and newspapers (Many require a few weeks of notice in order to list the event!)
 Distribute Fliers/Handouts everywhere and as soon as possible
 Establish a hashtag (ex: #ATXCrypto) and use it

Software

 Put applicable software onto flash drives to be used throughout presentations
 Inform audience of software to be used. You can also recommend pre-installation of this software in your e-mail, on a website or social media, etc.
 Consider having a “pre-party installfest” roughly 30 minutes before the event to install relevant software so that participants need not waste any valuable learning time during the presentations acquiring the software

Supplies

 AC power strips, extension cords (if needed)
 Projector, Screen, Amplified Sound
 Mailing List sign-up sheet
 Tip jar / Link to fundraising to help continue supporting events like these
 Loot - stickers, buttons, flyers for other events, postcards, linux .iso’s, etc.

SCHEDULE

InstallFest

 Download and install applicable software at event (when applicable to individual audience members)

Presenters

 Designate the order, content, and duration of presentations
 Presentations should show both why and how encryption is used
 If applicable, upload or distribute slides and handouts before party so that people may follow along

Break-Out sessions

 Break into groups after the presentation. This allows for more focused instruction for different operating systems or computer skill levels.
 Assign “experts” for these break-outs to guide or moderate the discussion
 Be sure to have enough “experts” who know the party’s topic to help explain it to the people learning it. Consider having an informal training session beforehand.
