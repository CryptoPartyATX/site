Privacy Plug-ins

Browser plug-ins to help protect your privacy as you browse the web.

Purpose: This guide suggests various plug-ins to enhance your privacy while you use web browsers such as Firefox or Chrome.

Software: The following plug-ins are used in this guide:

HTTPS Everywhere

Makes secure connections to any supported website that you visit.
https://www.eff.org/https-everywhere

Adblock Plus

Blocks advertisements from most pages, reducing annoyance and tracking.
https://adblockplus.org

Disconnect

Blocks third-party tracking by Facebook, Twitter, Google, and others.
https://disconnect.me

Ghostery

Blocks third-party tracking by various sites.
https://www.ghostery.com

StartPage Search

Adds StartPage to your search bar to make private searches more convenient.
https://startpage.com/eng/download-startpage-plugin.html

Cryptocat

Provides encrypted chat rooms and allows encrypted private messages
https://crypto.cat/

Nutshell

  1. First, we talk about why these plug-ins are useful.
  2. Second, we briefly discuss installation. (They're all easy!)

Part I: Why use these plug-ins?

You are being tracked

Most free websites aren't really free. If you're not paying for access to a website, someone else usually is. Most of the time, that means advertisers. Companies like Facebook and Google spend millions of dollars on hardware, software, and labor to provide you with services. Those millions of dollars come from the advertisers who pay to place their ads on the sites.

These companies aren't just paying for exposure, though. The real value is in tracking your behavior, mostly for the purpose of providing "targeted ads" that are shown to people who are most likely to click them. For example, if you make a lot of Google searches about dogs, you will be more likely to see ads for dog food, even on other websites.

While this may seem benign, it has troubling consequences for your privacy. Many websites track all of the data you give them—emails, searches, "Likes", pages viewed, links clicked, etc. They collect this information to form a profile of your habits, and over time they can figure out things about you that you never even told them.

With all of this data about your habits at their fingertips, your privacy is at risk because you do not have any control over where this information goes. It could be sold to advertisers, given to partner companies, pilfered from company servers, or handed over to government agencies.

How do these plug-ins help?

Adblock Plus, Disconnect, and Ghostery block certain parts of webpages from loading. This prevents advertisers and other third parties from collecting data about you when you do not visit them directly. For example, this will not prevent Facebook from tracking your activity on their website, but it will prevent them from tracking what you do on other websites that have a Facebook plug-in.

HTTPS Everywhere ensures that you use an encrypted connection as often as possible, which helps prevent your ISP or anyone else monitoring your connection from determining what you are looking at. With an https:// connection it will still be possible for them to see what sites you are in contact with, but they will not be able to determine what pages you load on those sites.

StartPage Search provides indirect Google search results over HTTPS. This means that Google cannot see who is making the original request, so they cannot associate those searches with your profile. Also, it allows you to optionally view a search result by proxy, so you do not connect to that website directly.

Cryptocat provides online chatrooms, similar to IRC, but the connection and content are encrypted. This means that only the people in that room will be able to read the messages, not anyone's ISP or other connection snoops. In addition, it allows the sending of private messages to users, providing roughly the equivalent privacy of encrypted IM chats (although you must ensure the person you are talking to is who they say they are).

Part II: Installing the Plug-ins

Before you begin

Before you install the plug-ins, you will need to ensure you have a browser that supports them.

  • We recommend Firefox because it is open-source and not created by Google.
  • Chrome is also open-source, but is made by a company that makes its money by selling your data to advertisers...
  • Plug-ins for Internet Explorer or Safari have not and will not be researched or recommended.

Installation

To install any of the plug-ins, click the links above and click the clearly marked download button on each page. Your browser will ask you to confirm you want to install the plug-in, and will notify you if you need to restart the browser before you can use it.

Configuration

  • Most of the software does not need to be configured.
  • HTTPS Everywhere and Adblock Plus may ask you a few questions, but all of the default options are okay.
  • When installing Ghostery, proceed through the setup wizard and be sure to click "Select all" on both the Trackers tab and the Cookies tab on the Blocking page.
  • You may need to edit your toolbars to hide or show the icons for these plug-ins.
